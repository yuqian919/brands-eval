How It All Started


by Andi, Flora & Nancy

With the hope to be socially-conscious with our own choices, we designed this website to provide comprehensive information on a variety of fashion brands, sourcing from verifiable third-party organizations. You the user can put in a brand name that you are interested in learning about into the search bar, and, if we have its information, you will be directed to a page with detailed descriptions on this brand's performance. Information includes aspects from environment impact, fair treatment of their workers , social impact, and how they prevent animal cruelty.

While you will see many brands that you are familiar with when exploring our website, you will also notice that there is incompleteness of information. This is because the idea of ethical consumerism is still new, in some level, to many people.



Resources We Used


Fashion Revolution    


Fashion Revolution is a UK-based social enterprise that promotes radical change in the way clothes are sourced, produced and consumed so that clothing is made in a safe, clean and fair way.  



    Tearfund NZ


Tearfund NZ is a New Zealand based aid agency that conducts their independent reports on global issues such as the refugee crisis, the unfair treatment of factory workers in the fashion industry, climate change.

Clean Clothes Campaign    


Clean Clothes Campaign is a global alliance dedicated to improving working conditions and empowering workers in the global garment and sportswear industries.    



    Shop Ethical!


Shop Ethical! is a shopping guide website that focus on recording fashion companies' track record surronding ethical consumerism issues. It integrates third-party datasets and reports to produce their evaluation.


Fashion Transparency Index 2017    


Fashion Transparency Index 2017 is a third-party report evaluating fashion clothing lines' transparency in regard to their policies.

Nancy

Nancy cares deeply about environment and is concerned with the unknown consequences of climate change. For her, designing this project is part of her plan to examine different aspects of her own carbon footprint with in mind the goal to reduce waste and pollution.

Andi

Andi gets her inspiration from a fashion brand project she did in OgilvyOne, an advertisement company. She loves the idea of bringing data into fashion field, which perfectly fits her interest in data science and marketing.

Flora

Flora started to explore more ecofriendly products two years ago and, when she has the oppurtunity to learn coding, she wanted to design a website where she can look up the ethical practice of clothing brands.

Ethical Consumer is a group project created in part for the completion of CAPP 30122 course at the University of Chicago. This website is not for commmericial use.
